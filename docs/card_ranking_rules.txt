This text file explains what card ranking rules are used in this game.

Poker hand ranking:
(sorted from least to greatest, an example is provided next to each kind)

0. High Card (A♠ J♦ 8♣ 6♠ 2♥)
High card is when you have five cards that do not interact with each other to make any of the below hands.

1. One-Pair (A♦ A♥ K♠ 9♦ 4♥)
One pair consists of two cards of the same value, and three extra cards.

2. Two-Pair (9♣ 9♦ 6♣ 6♠ Q♥)
​​Two pair consists of two cards of equal value, another two cards of equal value, and one extra card.

3. Three-of-a-Kind (7♠ 7♦ 7♣ K♦ Q♣)
Three of a kind is 3 cards of the same value and 2 side cards of different values.

4. Straight (9♥ 8♠ 7♣ 6♦ 5♣)
A straight has 5 cards of consecutive value that are not all the same suit.

5. Flush (A♠ J♠ 8♠ 4♠ 3♠)
A flush is a hand which has all cards of the same suit.

6. Full House (A♥ A♣ A♦ 9♠ 9♣)
A full house consists of three cards of one value and two cards of another.

7. Four-of-a-Kind (J♦ J♣ J♠ J♥ K♦)
Four of a kind consists of four cards of equal value along with another card known as a side card.

8. Straight Flush (10♥ 9♥ 8♥ 7♥ 6♥)
A straight flush consists of any straight that is all the same suit.

9. Royal Flush (A♦ K♦ Q♦ J♦ 10♦)
A royal flush consists of A, K, Q, J and 10, all of the same suit.


Note that if more than one hand is tied at the same poker hand, refined comparison will be used.
